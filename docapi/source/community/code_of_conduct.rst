Code of Conduct
===============

Contributor Covenant 3.0 Code of Conduct
----------------------------------------

Our Pledge
~~~~~~~~~~

We pledge to make our community welcoming, safe, and equitable for all.

We are committed to fostering an environment that respects and promotes the
dignity, rights, and contributions of all individuals, regardless of
characteristics including race, ethnicity, caste, color, age, physical
characteristics, neurodiversity, disability, sex or gender, gender identity or
expression, sexual orientation, language, philosophy or religion, national or
social origin, socio-economic position, level of education, or other status.

The same privileges of participation are extended to everyone who participates
in good faith and in accordance with this Covenant.

Encouraged Behaviors
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

While acknowledging differences in social norms, we strive to meet our
community's expectations for positive behavior.

We understand that words and actions may be interpreted differently based on
culture, background, or native language.

We agree to:

#. Respect the purpose of our community, our activities, and our ways of
   gathering.
#. Engage kindly and honestly with others.
#. Respect different viewpoints and experiences.
#. Take responsibility for our actions and contributions.
#. Give and accept constructive feedback.
#. Commit to repairing harm when it occurs.
#. Promote and sustain the well-being of our community.

Restricted Behaviors
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Instances, threats, and promotion of the following behaviors are violations of
this Code of Conduct.

#. **Harassment.** Violating explicitly expressed boundaries or engaging in
   unnecessary personal attention after a clear request to stop.
#. **Character attacks.** Making insulting, demeaning, or pejorative comments
   directed at a community member or group.
#. **Stereotyping or discrimination.** Characterizing anyone's personality or
   behavior based on immutable identities or traits.
#. **Sexualization.** Behaving in a way considered inappropriately intimate in
   the context or purpose of the community.
#. **Violating confidentiality.** Sharing or acting on someone's personal or
   private information without permission.
#. **Endangerment.** Causing, encouraging, or threatening violence or harm
   toward any person or group.
#. Behaving in other ways that threaten the well-being of the community.

Other Restrictions
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Additional restricted behaviors include:

#. **Misleading identity.** Impersonating someone else or pretending to be
   someone else to evade enforcement actions.
#. **Failing to credit sources.** Not properly crediting sources of contributed
   content.
#. **Promotional materials.** Sharing marketing or commercial content outside
   community norms.
#. **Irresponsible communication.** Presenting content that includes, links, or
   describes restricted behaviors irresponsibly.

Reporting an Issue
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Conflicts can occur between community members even when everyone is trying to
collaborate.

Not every conflict represents a Code of Conduct violation, but this Code
establishes behaviors and norms that help avoid conflicts and minimize harm.

To report a possible violation, please contact the project maintainers:

**Email:** zouariomar20@gmail.com

Community moderators take reports seriously and will make every effort to
respond in a timely manner.

Reports will be investigated by reviewing relevant information, including
messages, logs, recordings, or discussions with involved participants.

Investigations and enforcement actions will be handled privately while
prioritizing safety and confidentiality.

Addressing and Repairing Harm
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

If an investigation determines that this Code of Conduct has been violated,
the following enforcement actions may be considered depending on the severity
and impact of the incident.

#. **Warning**

   * Event: A violation involving a single incident or series of incidents.
   * Consequence: A private written warning from community moderators.
   * Repair: Written apology, acknowledgement of responsibility, and
     clarification of expectations.

#. **Temporarily Limited Activities**

   * Event: Repeated violations or a first serious violation.
   * Consequence: A temporary cooldown period limiting certain activities or
     interactions.
   * Repair: Reflection on actions, impact, and responsible participation after
     the cooldown period.

#. **Temporary Suspension**

   * Event: Continued violations or a serious single violation.
   * Consequence: Temporary removal from community spaces with conditions for
     return.
   * Repair: Respecting suspension requirements and demonstrating commitment to
     improved behavior.

#. **Permanent Ban**

   * Event: Severe violations or repeated violations that cannot be resolved.
   * Consequence: Removal from all community spaces, tools, and communication
     channels.
   * Repair: No possible repair in cases of this severity.

This enforcement ladder is a guideline and does not limit the ability of
community managers to use judgment based on the best interests of the
community.

Scope
~~~~~

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces.

It also applies when an individual officially represents the community in
public or other spaces, including:

* Using an official email address.
* Posting through official social media accounts.
* Acting as an appointed representative at events.
